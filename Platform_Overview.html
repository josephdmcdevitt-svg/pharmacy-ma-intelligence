Pharmacy Acquisition Intelligence Platform — Overview

This document explains the data sources, scoring methodology, and intended use of the Pharmacy Acquisition Intelligence Platform. The platform is designed to be used by retail pharmacy chains (e.g., Walgreens, CVS, regional independents) to identify independent pharmacy acquisition targets using publicly available federal data — no estimates or fabricated metrics.

Key Principle: Every data point in the platform comes from a real, verifiable federal source. There are no estimated prescription volumes, no fabricated file values, and no made-up metrics. What you see is what the government has published.

2. What Each Source Tells You

NPPES (NPI Registry) — The Foundation

This is the master list of every pharmacy in the United States. Every pharmacy that bills Medicare/Medicaid must have an NPI number. The platform uses this to:

  • Identify every independent pharmacy — by cross-referencing organization names against known chain patterns (CVS, Walgreens, Walmart, etc.) and flagging multi-location operators
  • Get owner/contact information — the "Authorized Official" is typically the pharmacy owner or managing pharmacist, with their direct phone number
  • Determine how long a pharmacy has been operating — the NPI enumeration date tells you exactly when they registered, so "years in operation" is calculated from real data
  • Detect closing signals — NPI deactivation dates show recently closed pharmacies, and "last update date" shows when the owner last touched their registration (stale = potentially disengaged owner)
  • Cross-reference with your internal systems — the NPI number is the universal pharmacy identifier that your claims systems, PBM contracts, and wholesaler records all use

CMS Medicare Part D — Real Prescription Volume

This is the single most valuable data source for pharmacy acquisitions. CMS publishes actual Medicare prescription claim data by NPI, including:

  • Total claim count — the real number of Medicare prescriptions filled at that pharmacy
  • Beneficiary count — how many unique Medicare patients use that pharmacy
  • Total drug cost — the actual dollar volume of drugs dispensed under Medicare
  • Brand vs. generic split — tells you about the pharmacy's dispensing patterns and potential margin profile
  • Opioid and antibiotic claims — useful for compliance assessment
Important: Medicare Part D data represents roughly 30-40% of a typical independent pharmacy's total prescription volume. Retail chains like Walgreens and CVS can cross-reference these Medicare claim counts against their own PBM data and third-party prescription data (IQVIA, Surescripts) to build a more complete picture of total volume.

Census ACS — Market Demographics

Demographics tell you about the market a pharmacy serves:

  • % population 65+ — seniors fill 2-3x more prescriptions than younger adults. A ZIP with 25%+ seniors is a high-value pharmacy market
  • Median income — higher income areas typically have better commercial payer mix (higher reimbursement rates)
  • % uninsured — high uninsured rates mean more cash-pay patients (lower volume but sometimes higher margin)
  • Population growth — growing markets mean the pharmacy's patient base is expanding naturally
  • % disabled — disabled populations have higher medication utilization

HRSA Shortage Areas — Competitive Moats

Pharmacies in HPSAs (Health Professional Shortage Areas) have a natural competitive advantage:

  • Fewer competitors in the area by definition
  • Higher patient need and medication utilization
  • Potential eligibility for 340B pricing (significant margin advantage)
  • Less likely to face new competitor entry

3. Acquisition Scoring Methodology

Every independent pharmacy receives a score from 0-100 based entirely on real, verifiable data:

Factor Weight Data Source Why It Matters for Acquisitions
Medicare Part D Claims 25% CMS Part D PUF Real prescription volume — higher claims = more valuable pharmacy. This is the closest public proxy to actual Rx volume.
Competition Density 20% NPPES (calculated) Pharmacies per 10,000 population in the ZIP. Fewer nearby pharmacies = easier to retain patients after acquisition.
Aging Population (65+) 15% Census ACS Seniors fill 2-3x more prescriptions. High 65+ percentage = high medication demand in the market.
Retirement Risk 15% NPPES Enumeration Date Pharmacies operating 25+ years = owner likely 60+ and approaching retirement. Most willing sellers.
HPSA Designation 10% HRSA Federally-designated shortage area = less competition, higher need, potential 340B eligibility.
Income / Payer Mix 8% Census ACS Higher median income = better commercial insurance penetration = higher reimbursement rates.
Population Growth 7% Census ACS Growing market = natural patient base expansion. The acquired pharmacy's value appreciates over time.

4. How to Use This With Your Existing Resources

The platform is designed to complement — not replace — the data and tools that retail pharmacy chains already have access to:

Cross-Reference with Internal Claims Data

  • NPI matching: Every pharmacy in the platform has an NPI number. Use this to match against your PBM claims data, wholesaler purchase records, and internal competitive intelligence
  • Medicare as a baseline: The Medicare Part D claims represent ~30-40% of typical independent volume. Your internal data can fill in commercial, Medicaid, and cash volume to get the full picture
  • Validate the score: A pharmacy scoring 80+ in our system that also shows high volume in your claims data is a double-confirmed high-value target

Combine with Third-Party Data

  • IQVIA / Symphony Health: Match NPIs to get total Rx volume (all payers), therapeutic mix, and market share data
  • Surescripts: Real-time prescription routing data can show current fill volumes
  • Wholesaler data (McKesson, AmerisourceBergen, Cardinal): Purchase volume by NPI gives you actual drug purchasing patterns
  • State Board of Pharmacy: License status, disciplinary actions, inspection history

Workflow Integration

  1. Identify targets — Use the platform's Top Targets and Query Tools to build a shortlist
  2. Enrich internally — Match NPIs against your claims/PBM data for total volume picture
  3. Prioritize outreach — Use Closing Signals (long-tenured, stale records) to identify most willing sellers
  4. Track deals — Use the Deal Pipeline to manage outreach status across the team
  5. Export lists — Every view has CSV export for loading into your CRM or outreach tools

For Tuck-in Acquisitions Specifically

The Tuck-in Finder lets you enter any store's ZIP code and find nearby independent pharmacies. This is designed for:

  • District managers looking for file acquisitions near existing stores
  • M&A teams evaluating geographic density plays
  • Market planning teams assessing competitive landscape by ZIP
